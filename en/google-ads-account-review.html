Google Ads Campaign structure, cost signals and search visibility

Why choose an account review

A Google Ads account review is useful when campaigns are already live but the next move is unclear. It helps identify whether budget is being used efficiently, whether tracking can be trusted and whether campaigns are aligned with real search intent.

External expert view

A fresh look can reveal structure issues, wasted spend or missed opportunities that are harder to see from inside the account.

Fast strategic clarity

This is designed as a quick audit, not a long engagement, so you can get useful answers before making more changes or increasing budget.

Clear next priorities

The goal is not to overload the account with minor tweaks, but to identify the few important fixes or decisions that matter most.

Which key metrics and elements matter

A useful review does not look at clicks in isolation. It connects visibility, cost, conversions and tracking quality to understand whether the account is actually positioned to generate qualified leads.

Cost per lead

Shows how much budget is required to generate a conversion such as a form submission or phone call. It helps judge efficiency, not just volume.

Conversion tracking

If tracking is weak or incomplete, every conclusion becomes less reliable. Validation of forms, calls and key actions is a core part of the review.

Impression share

Helps show whether the account is visible enough in relevant searches or whether budget and rank limitations are restricting reach.

Click-through rate

CTR helps indicate whether ads are relevant and appealing for the searches being targeted. Weak CTR can signal poor alignment or weak messaging.

Cost per click

CPC helps reveal how expensive the market is and whether campaign structure or targeting may be forcing the account into costly traffic unnecessarily.

Search intent alignment

Keywords, search terms and exclusions are reviewed to see whether the account is attracting the right searches rather than simply buying traffic.

What this review is built to check

Campaign structure

Whether campaigns and ad groups are logically organized around brand, high intent themes, services or product categories.

Budget focus

Whether spend appears concentrated on the searches and campaigns most likely to generate qualified leads rather than diluted across weak areas.

Tracking reliability

Whether the account appears to measure meaningful actions clearly enough to support real optimization decisions.

Practical priorities

Whether the next step should be cleanup, tighter targeting, better tracking, message refinement or stronger landing page alignment.
