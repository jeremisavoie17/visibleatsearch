What is Google Ads

Unlike social advertising, which interrupts users while they browse, Google Ads captures existing demand by appearing when people actively search for products or services.

Search campaigns

Capture of high-intent searches from people actively looking for products or services.

Performance Max campaigns

Expansion of reach across Google while supporting search demand coverage.

Intent targeting

Use of keywords, exclusions, and audiences to focus campaigns on the most relevant clicks.

Which metrics matter

Understanding indicators such as cost per lead (CPL), cost per click (CPC), click-through rate (CTR), and impression share helps identify whether campaigns are reaching the right searches and using budget efficiently.

Cost per lead (CPL)

The cost required to generate a form submission, phone call, or qualified lead.

Cost per click (CPC)

The price paid for each click generated by your ads.

Click-through rate (CTR)

The percentage of users who click after seeing your ad.

Impression share (IS)

The percentage of eligible auctions where your ads appear in search results.

Frequently Asked Questions

A Google Ads specialist builds and manages advertising campaigns to generate qualified traffic, leads, or sales. This includes keyword research, campaign structure, bidding strategy, conversion tracking, and ongoing optimization.

Google Ads costs vary depending on keyword competition, industry, and geography. Businesses control their own daily budget and only pay when someone clicks on an ad.

Google Ads can start generating traffic as soon as campaigns go live. Stable performance usually takes more time as data is collected and campaigns are optimized.

Many small businesses can launch ads on their own, but campaign structure, keyword targeting, and conversion tracking are often what determine whether budget is used efficiently. A Google Ads consultant helps improve that foundation.

Google Ads conversion tracking measures actions such as form submissions, phone calls, or purchases generated by advertising campaigns. It helps advertisers optimize toward real business outcomes instead of simple clicks. A proper setup can also connect with Google Analytics (GA4) and Google Tag Manager (GTM) for stronger measurement.

SEO improves visibility in organic search results over time, while Google Ads allows businesses to appear immediately in sponsored placements by paying for clicks.

The most useful Google Ads metrics often include cost per lead, cost per click, click-through rate, impression share, and conversion tracking. Together, these metrics help evaluate efficiency, visibility, and lead quality.
