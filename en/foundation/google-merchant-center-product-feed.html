What is Google Merchant Center

Google Merchant Center is the platform used to submit and manage product data for Google. It helps businesses show products in Shopping ads and product-based campaign formats connected to Google Ads.

Product feed

Send structured product data such as titles, prices, images, availability, and landing pages to Google.

Approvals & diagnostics

Resolve issues caused by policy problems, missing attributes, broken pages, or price mismatches.

Store connection

Connect product data with store information to enable local inventory and location-based listings.

Which elements matter in a product feed

Google Merchant Center performance depends on a few key feed elements that affect product approval, search relevance, and campaign readiness.

Fields such as product title, price, availability, image quality, landing page accuracy, and feed freshness help determine whether products are eligible and how well they can perform.

Product title

Clear, accurate titles help Google understand what the product is and when it should appear.

Price and availability

Accurate pricing and stock status must match the landing page to avoid feed issues and disapprovals.

Product image

Strong product images improve feed quality and help listings appear more complete and trustworthy.

Landing page match

The product page must reflect the same key information shown in the feed, including price and availability.

Feed freshness

Regular updates help keep product data accurate as inventory, prices, and page content change over time.
