What is Google Merchant Center

Google Merchant Center is the platform used to submit and manage product data to Google. It allows businesses show products in Shopping ads and product-based campaign formats connected to Google Ads.

Product feed

Submission of structured product data to Google such as titles, prices, images, availability, and landing pages.

Approvals & diagnostics

Resolution of issues related to policy requirements, missing attributes, broken pages, or price mismatches.

Store connection

Association of product data with store information to enable local inventory features.

Key product feed elements

Google Merchant Center performance depends on a few key feed elements that affect product approval, search relevance, and campaign readiness. Regular updates also help keep product data accurate as inventory, prices, and page content change over time.

Product title

Clear, accurate titles help Google understand what the product is and when it should appear.

Price and availability

Accurate pricing and stock status must match the landing page to avoid feed issues and disapprovals.

Product image

Strong product images improve feed quality and help listings appear more complete and trustworthy.

Landing page match

The product page must reflect the same key information shown in the feed, including price and availability.
